SAT scores by school district, 2016-17

Connecticut began having all students take the SAT test, referred to as "universal" SAT testing, in 2015-16. The second year of data, for 2016-17, is out now. Below you can search for your district and see how many students are considered college- and career-ready and severely behind, as well as which direction scores moved from one year to the next. The bars next to each district's name represent math and ELA scores on an 800-point scale.
= Avg. score changed less than 20 points;
= Avg. score increased at least 20 points;
= Avg. score decreased at least 20 points;
= 75% or more students were college/career ready in math or ELA;
= 25% or more were severely behind.
